ABOUT

Satamraju Satyanayarana Rao , fondly called Satya is a social activist who believes in “service to country is not just my exposition but my mission and passion“. Satya is currently working as a Business analysts in a global enterprise. He also volunteers in various social organizations for developing a better India.

Birth & Education

Born in a Brahmin family on April 25 1988 in Ongole, Andhra Pradesh to Sri S L Chenakesava Rao and Smt. Somaraju Sita Aruna.Satya was destined to serve nation at an early age of his life . He attended schooling in Guntur district before completing his Intermediate Education (Higher Secondary) at AKVK Jr. College ,Ongole through Andhra Pradesh Board of Intermediate Education. He pursued Bachelor’s degree in Commerce with specialization in Computers at Sri Harshini Degree college, Ongole. Currently, Satya is pursuing his MBA in finance at Dravidian University, a State University of Andhra Pradesh, along with his social services pursuits.

Sojourn to social life

Satya was introduced to RSS at a young age of 5 by his father. It’s ideology and teachings attracted him into nationalism and transformed the young mind to serve the society. Being part of the organization since a young age has made him to interact with eminent leaders of various streams /backgrounds and firmed his mind to work towards betterment of the society. As a swayam sevak (RSS) since childhood, Satya would combine his ancient values and modern requirements in solving the problems existing in the society. His optimism and leadership qualities was demonstrated in a 2004 incident in Ongole where he was part of the team to conduct “Dharma Sammelanam”. It was a ritual to be perform by 200 dalit families of Dattareya colony. The successful conduct of the ritual not only earned him appreciations from dalit community but also groomed his way for a visionary leader under RSS.
As a RSS swayam sevak Satya actively participated in rehabilitation of 2005 tsunami victims.
He travelled extensively along with the activists to Ongole villages educating villagers about Sethu Samudram Project and over protection of heritage property. He was detained in the protest for the above cause along with other Kar sevaks. Satya also participated in the protest against kanchi pontiff and was taken under preventive detention .
As a full time RSS pracharak he worked for many fishing villages of Prakasam district namely Chellemmagiripalem, Wollapalem and Pakala.
Working under RSS he understood the social stigma existing in the society, the discrimination among castes/communities, various existing evil practices and strengthened himself to work for their betterment in the society. He worked in area bastis to improve quality of life for the people living there. Satya devoted himself in addressing their issues and grievances. He even broke bread with dalit families in their homes to instill confidence among them to be part of the comprehensive development.
His volunteering services came to a halt with sudden demise of his father. He had to take up financial and household responsibilities at a young age of 18. The death of his father made him face the harsh reality of our existing social system. Having been born in a upper caste Brahmin family, Satya could not avail the financial scholarship to continue his studies, as they were meant only for the socially backward of the society as reservations and scholarship were primarily available to lower castes (who were stigmatized in the past and mandal commission introduced by then PM V P Singh (1990 August 7 ) to allow them in mainstream.)
This phase of personal hardships exposed him to similar stories of many Brahmin families and youth who were suffering and leading a life of poverty, a side effect of reservations which was boon for many but bane for families like them. Determined to empower the Brahmin youths Satya started a organization called Brahmin Yuva Kendra in to help Brahmin youth by skill trainings. The organization was formally registered in 2010 and is successfully helping the needy youth

Activities done through BrahminYuva kendra :

  • Organized 46 events which includes spiritual & youth conventions and dharma awareness camps like 'Brahmana Chaitanya Sadassu', 'Vipra', 'Shankara Jayanthi', 'Vipra Inspire', 'Swami Vivekananda's 150th Birth Anniversary Celebrations' by eminent people
  • Conducted many Dharma Sammelans and Youth awareness camps within the short span of 2 years i.e., from 2011 to 2013
  • Participated in “Vimochan Divas" conducted by Pragna Bharathi and YMIS.
  • Gave representation in many seminars conducted by various organizations like Social Cause, Pragna Bharathi, and Hindu Devaalaya Parirakshana Samithi.
  • Conducted and participated in meetings to condemn the harsh arrest of Shri Kamalananda Bharathi.
  • Participated in the seminar on Vedic Educational System conducted by Brahma Vidya Kutir.
  • Assisted many youngsters to secure jobs by providing references in various fields.
  • Conducted career guidance camps for many poor students by providing interaction with professionals.
  • Donated blankets to poor and needy that slept thread-less on roads during cold winters.
  • Donated blood whenever needed and participated in health camps conducted by Seva Bharathi.
  • Represented the Brahmin Community to the Governor along with then Health Minister Sri D. Sridhar Babu & other Brahmin organizations & gave the memorandum against the movies- “Women in Brahmanism” & “Denikaina Ready” .

Satya’s hard work towards socially backward sections and ability to reach out to help others earned him respect among seniors leaders. He is now part of many intellectual forums like - Pragna Bharath and Social Change
In 2014, Satya was appointed as State manager of Brahmin Welfare Corporation formed by Govt. of Andhra Pradesh.
Satya’s unique method of planning made him part of national level convention of the Brahmin community held at Hyderabad, 2015.
As a board member Satya toured six districts of Andhra Pradesh namely Prakasam, Guntur, Kurnool, Kadapa, Ananthpur, Chitoor and submitted a comprehensive report on Brahmin community’s financial and social needs. Based on these surveys the AP Brahmin Welfare Corporation has formulated various schemes which have impacted around 16000 students.
As a volunteer of AOL (Art of Living) Satya volunteered it’s “Project Soberman” for addressing the menace of alcoholism and drug addiction along the lines of PM Sri Narendra Modi's call for “drug free India”, which also involved celebrities, bureaucrats and public representatives, to name a few - MLA Sri G Kishan Reddy, former DGP K. Aravinda Rao, Indian Idol winner Ram Charan, TV anchor Suma and many more.
Satya’s commitment to social services led to his association with many intellectual forums apart from RSS, AOL, like Pragna Bharat, Social Cause, Hindu Devalaya Pariraksha Samiti, Virat Hindustan Sangam,etc.
Currently, Satya is working as a Business Analyst for a reputed IT firm besides continuing his social services.
He is an avid reader and practices yoga regularly.
Satya’s areas of interest includes - Politics, Economics, Public policies and governance, citizen centric initiatives, preservation of cultural heritage, Social empowerment: women, youth.
